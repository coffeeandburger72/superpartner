# Gifts Builder

You are generating a structured `gifts.md` gift-ideas file for a partner. Unlike other builders, this one reads previously generated profile files — not raw analyzer output. The profile files have already been written to disk.

Your job is to read those files, synthesize their information, and produce a well-reasoned, evidence-backed gift guide.

---

## Step 1: Identify the Partner

Determine the partner's slug (lowercase, hyphen-separated name — e.g., `jane-doe`) from context.

## Step 2: Read the Profile Files

Use the Read tool to read **all three** of the following files before generating any output:

- `partners/<slug>/persona.md`
- `partners/<slug>/interests.md`
- `partners/<slug>/occasions.md`

Replace `<slug>` with the actual partner slug. Do not proceed until all three files have been read.

**After reading, extract these key signals from `persona.md`:**

- **Love Language Ranking** (from Emotional Patterns → Love Language Ranking) — record the full ranking #1 through #5 with evidence summaries
- **Attachment Orientation** (from Attachment Orientation → Style) — record the classification and key behavioral signals
- **Activation Triggers** (from Attachment Orientation → Activation Triggers) — record situations that trigger insecurity or withdrawal

These signals directly inform gift prioritization and the Avoid section.

## Step 3: Determine the Next Occasion

From `occasions.md`, identify the single most upcoming occasion within the next 90 days (birthday, anniversary, trip, holiday, etc.) that warrants a gift or gesture. This becomes the "Next Occasion" section header.

If no upcoming occasion is identified in the 90-day window, use "General / Anytime" as the header instead.

## Step 4: Determine the Currency

Infer the local currency from evidence in the profile files (location hints, restaurant names, brand mentions, price discussions). Default order of inference:
- Explicit currency symbols or codes mentioned (HKD, TWD, RMB, USD, etc.)
- City or country context (Hong Kong → HKD, Taiwan → TWD, Mainland China → RMB, etc.)
- If ambiguous, default to USD and note the assumption

Use the detected currency for **all** budget estimates throughout the file.

## Step 5: Build the Gift Lists

Apply these rules before writing anything:

**Traceability rule:** Every gift must be traceable to a specific interest, preference, personality trait, or explicit mention from the profile files. Do not include generic gifts with no supporting evidence.

**Evidence hierarchy:**
1. Wishlist items (flagged ⚠️ HIGH VALUE in `interests.md`) → always High Confidence
2. Explicit likes/quotes → High Confidence
3. Inferred interests or behavioral patterns → Medium Confidence
4. Personality and lifestyle fit (from `persona.md`) → Medium Confidence or Experience/Personalized

**Avoid section:** This section is mandatory and critical. Pull from:
- Explicit dislikes or avoidances in `interests.md`
- Lifestyle constraints (pets → avoid strong scents; homebody → avoid high-commitment activities)
- The "she buys things she likes" pattern — if her profile suggests she purchases things she wants for herself, her gifts should be things she *wouldn't* buy (too expensive, too indulgent, not on her radar)
- Any sensitivities mentioned in `persona.md`

**Key Insight:** Write two paragraphs — first synthesizing her gift personality (reference her own words, especially quotes about how she shops or receives gifts; identify patterns: practical vs. sentimental, experiences vs. objects, surprises vs. asked-for), then covering love language strategy (see detailed rules in the Key Insight section below).

## Step 5b: Apply Love Language Prioritization

After building all gift lists, apply love language-based ordering:

**Love Language Tags:**
- QT = Quality Time (experiences, shared activities, dedicated attention)
- WA = Words of Affirmation (handwritten notes, personalized messages, engraved items)
- RG = Receiving Gifts (the item itself matters — curated, specific, desired)
- AS = Acts of Service (doing something for her — assembling, organizing, booking, planning)
- PT = Physical Touch (comfort items, wearables, things that evoke closeness)

**Ordering rule:** Within each confidence tier (High, Medium, Experience, Personalized), reorder rows so that gifts tagged with her top 2 love languages appear first, and those tagged with her bottom 2 appear last. This is a recommendation signal, not a filter — no gifts are removed.

**Multiple tags:** Some gifts serve multiple love languages. Tag with the primary one. Note secondary in the Why column if relevant.

## Step 6: Write the File

Use the Write tool to create `partners/<slug>/gifts.md` with exactly the structure below.

## Rules

### Traceability
- Every gift entry must include a "Why" that references a specific fact from the profile files.
- Do not include an item just because it is a common gift. Tie it to evidence.

### Budget Estimates
- Use the currency detected in Step 4 for all prices.
- Provide a range (e.g., HKD 200–400) rather than a single number when appropriate.
- For Experience Gifts, the budget is per-person unless noted otherwise.

### High Confidence Section
- Wishlist items flagged ⚠️ HIGH VALUE in `interests.md` must appear here.
- Items she has explicitly said she wants, likes, or has been "thinking about" belong here.
- The "Reference" column must contain a direct quote from the profile file (in the original language — do not translate).

### Medium Confidence Section
- Items supported by inferred interests or behavioral patterns (not direct quotes).
- The "Reasoning" column should describe the inference chain (e.g., "photographed matcha 4 times across Jan–Mar").

### Experience Gifts
- Prioritize experiences aligned with her food preferences, travel style, or social patterns.
- If she mentioned a specific restaurant, venue, or destination, reference it in the Notes column.
- Avoid experiences that conflict with her schedule, social comfort level, or lifestyle constraints.

### Personalized / Handmade
- Consider the *sender's* skills and hobbies if mentioned anywhere in the chat or profile.
- Focus on what makes the gift specific to *her* — not just a generic handmade item.
- "Effort" column should give an honest sense of time or skill required (e.g., "2–3 hours, basic cooking skills").

### Avoid Section
- This section is mandatory. If there are no explicit dislikes, infer from lifestyle context.
- Common inferences to check: pets (avoid strong scents/candles), minimalist personality (avoid clutter), she shops for herself (avoid things she'd easily buy), dietary restrictions (avoid food gifts that conflict).
- Quote the source evidence in the "Why Not" column where possible (original language).
- **Attachment-aware avoidances:** Read the attachment orientation from persona.md and add:
  - If anxious-leaning: avoid gifts that imply distance, independence, or separation (solo travel vouchers, "time for yourself" gifts, items that suggest spending time apart). Frame as: "she values togetherness — gifts emphasizing solo experiences may not land well."
  - If avoidant-leaning: avoid gifts that feel like commitment pressure or excessive intimacy escalation (matching couple items she didn't request, future-planning gifts like "our first home" items, overly sentimental items early in the relationship). Frame as: "she values her independence — gifts that feel like obligations may create discomfort."
  - If secure: no attachment-specific avoidances needed.
  - Frame all avoidances as practical observations about her preferences, never as psychological labels.

### General Anytime Gifts
- These should be genuinely versatile — items or experiences she'd welcome on any occasion.
- All items must still trace back to profile evidence.
- Use bullet points with brief reasoning, not tables.
- Include at least two items per price tier; write `(nothing identified at this tier)` only if there is truly no evidence to support any item.

### Key Insight
- This is the most important section for the human reading the file. Write it well.
- The first paragraph covers her gift personality: if the profile contains a quote about how she buys things she likes herself (e.g., "好鍾意嘅應該會自己買咗🤣除非太貴"), cite it directly and draw the implication. Describe whether she prefers practical vs. sentimental, experiences vs. objects, surprises vs. asked-for.
- The second paragraph covers love language strategy: "Her primary love language is [X], followed by [Y]. This means [specific implication for gift strategy — e.g., 'experience gifts and shared activities will land harder than expensive objects' or 'a heartfelt handwritten note alongside any gift will matter more than the gift itself']. Her lowest-ranked language is [Z], so [what to deprioritize — e.g., 'purely practical gifts without emotional context will feel impersonal']."
- Both paragraphs must cite specific evidence from the profile — quotes, patterns, or love language ranking.

## Step 7: Offer Concrete Picks (Web Search — Opt-In)

After writing the gift guide, offer the user:

> "I can search for specific products and experiences near [detected location from profile] based on these recommendations. Want me to look up concrete options with pricing?"

**If the user declines:** Skip this step entirely.

**If the user accepts:**

1. **Determine location:** Use location hints from persona.md and interests.md (city, district, restaurant names, brand mentions). If ambiguous, ask the user.
2. **Search for High Confidence + Wishlist items:** For each, search for where to buy locally or online, with pricing in the detected currency.
3. **Search for Experience Gifts:** Find real bookable experiences near the user — specific workshops, classes, restaurants, venues.
4. **Anchor results to profile:** For each search result, note why this specific product/venue fits her profile. Do not just list top search results.
5. **Append to the file:** Use the Edit tool to add a new section at the bottom of `gifts.md`:

```markdown
## Concrete Picks (searched on <date>)

> These are real products and venues found via web search. Availability and pricing may change.

### Products
| Item | Where | Price | Why This One |
|------|-------|-------|-------------|
| [specific product] | [store/URL] | [price in local currency] | [ties to profile — e.g., "matches her stated preference for X"] |

### Experiences
| Experience | Venue | Price | Why This One |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------------|
| [specific experience] | [venue name, location] | [price] | [ties to profile] |
```

**Rules:**
- Web search is opt-in only — never auto-triggered
- Search results must be anchored to profile evidence
- Use the detected currency for all pricing
- Separate from the main gift guide (appended section) so profile-based recommendations stay independent of time-sensitive search results
