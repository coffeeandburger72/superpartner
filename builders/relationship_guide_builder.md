# Relationship Guide Builder

You are generating a structured `relationship_guide.md` coaching document from persona analysis and relationship analysis results. This file synthesises everything known about the couple's dynamic into actionable, evidence-based guidance.

Unlike other builders that focus on one dimension (gifts, dates, occasions), this builder reads across the full profile to produce a holistic relationship coaching document.

---

## Step 1: Gather Input

Use the Read tool to load the following file. Replace `<slug>` with the actual partner slug.

```
partners/<slug>/persona.md
```

Additionally, the relationship analysis results (Dimensions 1-7) should be held in context from the create flow. If not in context (e.g., during guide regeneration), derive insights from persona.md — specifically:
- Communication Style (Layer 2)
- Emotional Patterns (Layer 3) — love language ranking, affection style, disagreement style
- Values & Boundaries (Layer 4)
- Attachment Orientation (Layer 5)

Also check context for:
- `ldr` flag (from intake Question 2 or auto-detected)
- Relationship stage and duration (from intake Question 2)

---

## Step 2: Identify the Partner

From the file contents and context, determine:
- Partner display name
- Partner slug
- Today's date in `YYYY-MM-DD` format
- Whether LDR mode is active
- Relationship stage (early / established / long-term)

Output file path: `partners/<slug>/relationship_guide.md`

---

## Step 3: Generate the Guide

Before writing, plan each section by anchoring every recommendation to specific evidence from the profile and analysis. Discard any tip or suggestion that cannot be traced to observed chat behavior.

**Tone rules:**
- Coaching, not clinical — "here's what works for you two" not "Subject A exhibits anxious tendencies"
- Never use psychological labels in headers, advice, or descriptions — behavioral language only
- Frame everything as observations and suggestions, not diagnoses
- "Concerning Patterns" uses neutral language: "This pattern appeared X times" not "This is a red flag"
- Written in the detected language of the chat (same as other profile files). Section headers stay in English.

## Section-by-Section Instructions

### Your Dynamic at a Glance

Write exactly 2-3 sentences. Each sentence must contain a specific claim backed by evidence:
- Sentence 1: Communication balance — who initiates, response patterns, volume
- Sentence 2: Affection style — how warmth flows, who expresses more, in what form
- Sentence 3: Conflict style — how tension surfaces and resolves

Do not write vague summaries like "You have a loving relationship with good communication."

### Attachment Dynamics

Pull directly from persona.md Layer 5 (Attachment Orientation). The "What She Needs" bullets translate behavioral signals into "do more of X" advice. The "What to Watch For" bullets list specific situations that trigger her activation patterns.

Rules:
- Never write "she has anxious attachment" — write behavioral descriptions
- Every bullet must cite evidence (a quote or observed pattern)
- Limit to 3 bullets each for "What She Needs" and "What to Watch For"

### Communication Patterns — Areas to Watch

Pull from Dimension 7 destructive pattern analysis. Only include patterns that were actually detected. For each:
- Describe the behavior, not the label (don't write "Criticism" as the header — write what the behavior looks like)
- Include exact quote evidence in original language
- "Suggested approach" must be specific to this couple's dynamic, not generic advice

If no destructive patterns were detected, this is a positive signal — say so.

### Communication Patterns — How to Raise Difficult Topics

Generate 3-5 tips. Each must:
- Be anchored to a specific observation from persona.md or relationship analysis
- Give a concrete "do this" recommendation, not just "be mindful of X"
- Cite the source signal in parentheses

**Good example:**
> She processes difficult conversations better in the evening — her messages are longer and more emotionally open after 9pm. If you need to discuss something serious, raise it during her evening window, not during her terse morning messages. (from persona.md — Communication Style → Tone by Context)

**Bad example:**
> Be honest and communicate openly.

### Emotional Connection (A.R.E.)

Pull from Dimension 7 A.R.E. assessment. For each dimension:
- State the score for both people
- Provide specific evidence (a quote showing responsiveness, or an instance of a missed emotional bid)
- "Strengthening the Bond" suggests specific actions for the lowest-scoring dimension

### Conflict & Repair

"Your Conflict Style" synthesises Dimension 5 findings into a couple-level pattern.

"Repair Playbook" is the most actionable section. Generate 3-5 repair strategies, each:
- Named with a descriptive action phrase (e.g., "Name the pattern before it escalates")
- Includes when to use it (specific trigger or situation from their dynamic)
- Includes example framing or approach (what to say or do — but not exact scripts, more like direction)
- Tied to evidence of what has or hasn't worked in their observed conflict episodes

### Relationship Health

"Positive Signals" — pull from Dimension 6 (future planning, vulnerability, topic diversity, engagement trend) and Dimension 7 (healthy A.R.E. scores, repair success). Include at least 3 concrete positive observations.

"Concerning Patterns" — pull from Dimension 7 destructive patterns. Use neutral language:
- "This pattern appeared X times across the chat history"
- "Both of you exhibit this in different contexts"
- Never: "This is a red flag" or "This is toxic behavior"

If no concerning patterns: state this clearly as a positive.

### Love Language Strategy

Pull ranking from persona.md Layer 3. "How to Apply This" translates each ranking into a specific daily behavior, not abstract advice.

**Good example:**
> Her #1 is Quality Time — she lights up when you suggest activities together and her most engaged messages follow shared experiences. Prioritize presence over presents: a 2-hour cafe afternoon beats an expensive gift she didn't ask for. (from persona.md — Love Language Ranking, interests.md — Wishlist patterns)

### Pro Tips

Generate 5-8 tips. Same rules as dating_ideas Pro Tips:
- Drawn from specific observations, not generic dating advice
- Actionable: "do this" or "avoid that"
- Cite source signal in parentheses
- Should synthesise across multiple profile dimensions — these are the highest-level insights

## Evidence and Language Rules

1. **Every recommendation must have a profile anchor.** No generic relationship advice. Every tip, suggestion, and observation must trace to specific evidence from persona.md or the relationship analysis.
2. **Keep quotes in original language.** Cantonese stays Cantonese, mixed-language stays mixed.
3. **Write in the detected language of the chat.** Section headers stay English.
4. **Do not invent information.** If a section has gaps, note them. Never fill gaps with assumptions.
5. **No psychological labels.** Describe behaviors, not diagnoses. "She checks in frequently when you're quiet" not "She has an anxious attachment style."
6. **Neutral framing for concerns.** "This pattern appeared X times" not "This is unhealthy." Present observations and let the user draw conclusions.
